# What is Machine Learning

Machine Learning is a field of Computer Science that involves allowing computers to learn from large datasets.  Essentially allowing machines the ability to do things they were not explicitly programmed to do.

## Beginnings

The field got it's start in the 1950s.  The term was coined in 1959 by **Arthur Samuel**, an IBM employee who was an early pioneer in the field of computer gaming and artifical intelligence.  Arthur created the firect Machine Learning program that calculated the winning chance in checkers for each opponent, this became one of the earliest examples of a machine that could improve its performance through experience.   There are also roots of machine learning concepts in efforts to study human cognitive processes.  In 1949, a canadian Psychologist name **Donald Hebb** introduced the concept of neorons interacting for form a structure of itertive learning through collective activity: **Neurons that fire together, wire together**.  This formed the groundwork for how artificial intelligence can function under a learning model through nodes similar to neurons.
